  <Section title="🐍 The Destiny That Could Have Been">
    Hello, wizards and witches! Welcome to _The Pensieve_. Today we are going to explore one of the most fascinating "What If" scenarios in the entire saga. We all remember the moment when Harry, under the Sorting Hat, whispered: <span style={{ color: "#928952", fontWeight: "bold" }}>"Not Slytherin, not Slytherin"</span>. But what would have happened if the Hat had ignored his request and shouted: <strong style={{ color: "#2E7D32" }}>SLYTHERIN!</strong>?
  </Section>

  <Section title="🤝 Friends or Foes? The Relationship with Draco Malfoy" imgSrc={mainImg} imgAlt="Harry Potter in Slytherin robes" loading="eager">
    If Harry had ended up in the snake's house, his first encounter with Draco Malfoy on the Hogwarts Express would have had a very different outcome. Instead of rejecting his hand, Harry would have found himself forced to <span style={{ color: "#928952" }}>navigate Slytherin politics</span> alongside him. Would they have become best friends, forming the most powerful duo in school, or would the internal rivalry for leadership have been even more fierce?
  </Section>

  <Section title="🧪 Snape and the Unexpected Favoritism">
    Severus Snape would have found himself in an <strong style={{ color: "#928952" }}>extremely uncomfortable</strong> position. As Head of Slytherin House, he would have to protect Harry while, at the same time, dealing with the resentment caused by seeing James Potter's face in the colors of his own house. Probably, Snape would have been <span style={{ color: "#928952", fontWeight: "bold" }}>a much more present mentor</span>, though just as stern, trying to keep Harry away from dark influences from within.
  </Section>

  <Section title="🌑 Voldemort's Influence">
    As a Slytherin, Harry would have direct access to the children of the Death Eaters. This could have made it easier for Voldemort to try to <strong style={{ color: "#928952" }}>recruit or manipulate him</strong>. However, it also means that Harry would know the secrets of his enemies much better than as a Gryffindor. The war would not have been fought only on battlefields, but in the corridors and common room of Slytherin.
  </Section>

  <Section title="⚖️ Would He Still Be the Hero?">
    Being in Slytherin doesn't make you evil, but it does enhance <span style={{ color: "#928952", fontWeight: "bold" }}>ambition and cunning</span>. Harry would still have his big heart, but his methods for defeating Voldemort would have been much more <strong style={{ color: "#928952" }}>calculating and pragmatic</strong>. Perhaps he wouldn't have needed so much luck, but more strategy.
  </Section>
